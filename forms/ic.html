Incident Commander

First Responder

The first responder will assess the situation (in Alberta, use the Alberta Assessment Matrix) and assign an Alert (AB only) or Level of Emergency to the incident. If an emergency is declared, the responders will coordinate duties and proceed with responsibilities for a Level One, Two or Three Emergency.

During the initial investigation phase of the response the first person on scene takes on the role of Incident Commander. This responsibility remains in effect until such time that a more senior supervisor arrives on site to relieve the first responder of Incident Command Duties.

NOTE:

This response plan is designed to maximize public safety. However, there could be alerts or emergencies that do not impact public safety, but are visible to members of the public. In these situations, the public may incorrectly perceive themselves to be at risk. If such an incident occurs, the Company will consider advising the public that there is an incident, but that it is not impacting public safety. If the Company chooses to contact the public, then the local petroleum regulatory agency, county, local authority will also be notified. Consideration should then be given to mobilize other response team members to provide additional assistance.

Incident Commander

The Incident Commander role should be assigned to the most experienced company supervisor or representative at the incident site. The Incident Commander has the responsibility to manage the on-scene activities and the implementation of a safe and effective tactical response.

The Incident Commander will:

  • In Alberta, use the Assessment Matrix (Section 4.1), in BC, use the Levels of Emergency) to assess the situation;
  • Classify the incident (Alert (AB only), Level 1 to Level 3);
  • Make appropriate internal and external notifications;
  • Establish an On-Site Command Post in a safe location;
  • Ensure the safety of response personnel and the public;
  • Ensure adequate planning is conducted;
  • Conduct a briefing session with Corporate staff member(s);
  • Work with Corporate staff to develop an Incident Action Plan;
  • Confirm there is an adequate communication link between command centres;
  • Initiate public communication and public safety response. (For Alberta incidents, use the Public Protection Decision Tree in Section 8.8);
  • Evaluate how many members of the public could be inside the emergency planning zone. Account for residents, businesses, First Nations persons, trappers, guide / outfitters, grazing lessees, transients, highways, waterways, railroads and public facilities;
  • Ensure the public who may perceive themselves to be at risk are informed about the emergency;
  • Initiate request for resources, personnel and equipment to address potential emergency situation;
  • Delegate responsibilities to Company personnel and contract support resources;
  • Establish a “Unified command" with government agencies at the incident site (if necessary); and
  • Notify Crisis Manager of any media requirements.

The Incident Commander should delegate responsibilities where possible.
