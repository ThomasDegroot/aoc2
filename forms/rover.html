Rover Evacuation Team

In conjunction with the Public Protection Team Director:

  • Review the boundaries of the response zones.
  • Evaluate how many members of the public could be inside the planning zone and the response zones. Account for residents, businesses, First Nations persons, trappers, guide / outfitters, grazing lessees, transients, highways, waterways, railroads, etc.
  • Prepare a response zones Rover responder safety action plan.
  • Prepare a response zones Rover public protection measure action plan.
  • Assess weather conditions in and around the area of the emergency. Determine if weather conditions could impact or impede emergency response efforts.
  • Mobilize the required number of Rovers. Prepare to dispatch.
  • If off-lease, advise members of the public inside the IIZ and residents identified as having special needs to evacuate. (If incident escalates, advise all members of other response zones to evacuate.) Provide evacuation assistance to anyone who requires it.
  • Identify and evacuate transients and any other members of the public within the response zones that may be unable to shelter-in-place if required to do so.
  • Post a Public Evacuation Notice on all doors of each unoccupied residence and business, on each outbuilding and the windshield of each unattended vehicle within the evacuated response zones.
  • Maintain security at evacuated residences / businesses and other property inside their Rover Area.
  • Take regular air quality readings and record results.
  • Regularly update the Public Protection Team Director about Rover activities inside response zones and results of air monitoring, if applicable.
  • DOCUMENT all activities.
