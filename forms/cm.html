Crisis Manager Resposibilities

The responsibility of the Crisis Manager is to establish the CEOC, coordinate the Calgary response staff and to provide support to the Incident Commander. The Crisis Manager will:

  • Receive an initial telephone call from the Incident Commander;
  • Ensure proper classification of the emergency (in Alberta, use the Alberta Assessment Matrix in BC, use the Levels of Emergency);
  • Determine what additional resources are required to support the Incident Commander;
  • Assess the incident situation with regards to both short and long term implications;
  • Ensure that there is no conflict or duplication of activities;
  • Ensure that all appropriate government authorities have been notified (ERCB / PEP / OGC, Municipal Government, and Occupational Health and Safety / WorkSafe BC);
  • Determine whether to establish the CEOC;
  • Contact additional resource personnel (Drilling Engineer, Facilities Engineer) as required;
  • Dedicate a phone line to the Incident Commander;
  • Review known information with CEOC staff and begin goal setting to bring incident under control;
  • Assess corporate responsibility with regards to health, environment, community and business impacts;
  • Assess potential for media interest at a Level 1 and the need to notify Corporate Spokesperson regarding a Media Release;
  • Assign a Corporate Regulatory Liaison to attend the Government Emergency Operations Centre (if necessary);
  • Provide guidance with public safety actions;
  • Conduct Joint Venture Partner notification
  • Consult with the ERCB / OGC to downgrade the Level of Emergency; and
  • Debrief with other CEOC members when the emergency has been downgraded.

Location: Corporate Emergency Operations Centre

Confers with Incident Commander and Gives Direction to:

  • Corporate Advisory Team Director;
  • Regulatory Liaison; and
  • Media / Corporate Spokesperson.
